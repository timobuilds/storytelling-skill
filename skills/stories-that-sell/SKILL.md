# Stories that Sell

## Doctrine

Read [../storyteller-tactics/references/doctrine.md](../storyteller-tactics/references/doctrine.md). Job: **convince people you can deliver** — stories beat spreadsheets for trust.

## Draft-first

Propose a filled [assets/sales-story-brief.md](assets/sales-story-brief.md) from context. Match density/shape of [assets/exemplar-sales-story-brief.md](assets/exemplar-sales-story-brief.md) (fictional gold sample — do not copy its facts). Mark unknowns `TBD`. Ask the user to react.

## Process

1. **Audience Profile** — who, problems, hopes, fears, what they already say/do. (Deep: `../storyteller-tactics/chapters/ch09-organise.md`)
2. **Simple Sales Stories** — “someone like you” and/or “someone you like”; Old / Regular / Tricky customer beats. (ch06)
3. **Social Proof** — trends (zoom out/in), prototypes, story-shaped testimonials. (ch04)
4. **Rags to Riches** — hidden value → struggle → deserved recognition; customer at the heart. If it sounds too good, switch to **No Easy Way**. (ch07)
5. **Pitch Perfect** — one-liner (What’s It About?) → elevator (problem / solve / trust) → **POPP**: Problem (−) → Opportunity (+) → Practical Steps (−) → Promise (+). (ch06)
6. **Style pass** — Movie-check; cap takeaways at three; log Story Bank permission.

## Output — Sales Story Brief

Fill [assets/sales-story-brief.md](assets/sales-story-brief.md), then a short **POPP pitch** the user can say aloud.

## Anti-patterns

- Pitching features with no Audience Profile
- Dissimilar customer stories with no bridge
- Rags to Riches only for sceptical buyers
- Boss saying “we’re great” when a customer should speak
