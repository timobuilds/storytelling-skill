# Stories that Convince

## Doctrine

Read [../storyteller-tactics/references/doctrine.md](../storyteller-tactics/references/doctrine.md). Job: **get non-experts to trust your judgment** without drowning them in data.

## Draft-first

Propose a filled [assets/convince-story-brief.md](assets/convince-story-brief.md). Match density/shape of [assets/exemplar-convince-story-brief.md](assets/exemplar-convince-story-brief.md) (fictional — don’t copy its facts). Mark `TBD`. Ask them to react.

## Process

1. **Three is the Magic Number** — pick ≤3 facts; Attention / Reversal / Goldilocks pattern. (ch08)
2. **That’s Funny** — the insight moment (ha-ha / peculiar / acting funny). (ch04)
3. **Data Detectives** — Zoomer, Detective, or Sceptic frame. (ch04)
4. **Trust Me, I’m an Expert** — values under cost. (ch05)
5. **Hero & Guide** — they are Hero; you are Guide with a gift. (ch05)
6. Style pass + permission notes.

## Output

Filled brief + a short “expert” story (≤2 min) ending in a clear ask.

## Anti-patterns

- Chart dump with no three
- Expertise claims with no character cost
- Making yourself the Hero of their journey
