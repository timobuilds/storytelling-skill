# Exemplar — Connect Story Brief (fictional)

Product / startup themed. Composite — not a real company.

```markdown
# Connect Story Brief
- Person / segment: “Alex,” Senior PM at a 60-person B2B SaaS evaluating Harbor. (Composite of 5 discovery interviews.)
- Permission to share: Alex OK with anonymized internal research; no company name externally.
- Strong emotion + why it sticks: Quiet dread Sunday nights — not of building product, of being the human API between eng reality and exec “green” dashboards.
- Timeline (goal → what happened): Goal: ship a priced feature by Friday. Tuesday: eng says the ticket is wrong. Schedule shows green. Reality: acceptance criteria lived in a Google Doc nobody linked. Friday slips; Alex gets the “visibility?” Slack from the CRO.
- Key decisions + counterfactuals: Almost rewrote the ticket at midnight (again). Didn’t — went to sleep angry. Counterfactual: if exceptions were first-class Tuesday morning, the rewrite happens in daylight with eng, not as unpaid weekend labor.
- Expertise a novice would miss: “In progress” ≠ “unblocked.” PMs track whose opinion can still kill the ship — not just Jira states.
- Observed action vs what they say:
  - Says: “I need better roadmap reporting.”
  - Does: Maintains a private Notion page of ‘real status’ they never show leadership.
- Universal trait in play: **Fear and bravery** — small act of protecting eng from performative status vs. protecting themselves from exec blame.
- Life stage / archetype (Circle of Life): **Adult** in the Wide World — past IC designer/PM comfort; not yet Parent/Head of Product. Conflict: Adult craft vs Parent theater.
- Empathy story draft (their shoes):
  “Alex doesn’t open another dashboard looking for charts. They open it scared of the one unspoken blocker that turns a green roadmap red on Friday. Every Sunday they rebuild the truth in a private Notion page the company tools can’t see. When Friday blows up, the roadmap still says fine — so the blame lands on the human who already did the unpaid translation work.”
- What this means for the product / decision: Harbor must make *exceptions* and *real blockers* first-class — the private Notion page is the product requirement.
- TBD: How many PMs keep a shadow status doc (survey n).
```
