# Patterns — Storyteller Tactics

## Story Building Diagnosis
**When to use**: Starting cold or stuck choosing a tactic.
**How**: Ask the seven questions; first "No" → that category (Concept → … → Organise).
**Trade-offs**: Fast routing vs skipping useful later categories — revisit after first draft.

## Desert Island Stack
**When to use**: Teaching, time pressure, or "learn seven cards only."
**How**: Dragon & City → Story Listening → Trust Me → Pitch Perfect → Man in a Hole → Movie Time → Story Bank.
**Trade-offs**: Breadth over specialized recipes.

## Recipe: Stories that Sell
**When to use**: Need trust and conversion.
**How**: Audience Profile → Simple Sales Stories → Social Proof → Rags to Riches → Pitch Perfect.
**Trade-offs**: Optimistic Rags to Riches may need No Easy Way for sceptics.

## Recipe: Stories that Motivate
**When to use**: Change adoption / leap of faith.
**How**: Dragon & City → Drive Stories → Three Great Conflicts → Innovation Curve → No Easy Way.
**Trade-offs**: Heavy prep; overkill for tiny asks.

## Recipe: Stories that Convince
**When to use**: Expert explaining to non-experts.
**How**: Three Magic Number → That's Funny → Data Detectives → Trust Me → Hero & Guide.
**Trade-offs**: Cuts data volume; pick the right three facts carefully.

## Recipe: Stories that Connect
**When to use**: Empathy for product, research, or leadership.
**How**: Story Listening → Abstractions → Universal Stories → Story-ish Conversations → Circle of Life.
**Trade-offs**: Slow; requires real listening time.

## Recipe: Stories that Explain
**When to use**: Strategy that nobody reads or cares about.
**How**: Order & Chaos → Good & Evil → What's It About? → Rolls Royce Moment → Story Hooks.
**Trade-offs**: Moral framing can polarize — use complex Good & Evil when needed.

## Recipe: Stories that Lead
**When to use**: Team culture and cohesion.
**How**: Curious Tales → Man in a Hole → Emotional Dashboard → Thoughtful Failures → Story Bank.
**Trade-offs**: Needs psychological safety for failure stories.

## Recipe: Stories that Impress
**When to use**: High-stakes presentation.
**How**: Movie Time → Five Ts → Story Hooks → Show & Tell → Cut to the Chase.
**Trade-offs**: Style without Concept/Explore = hollow polish.

## POPP Pitch Arc
**When to use**: Any pitch needing drama.
**How**: Problem (−) → Opportunity (+) → Practical Steps (−) → Promise (+).
**Trade-offs**: More theatrical than a flat elevator; still need Trust Me.

## Man in a Hole
**When to use**: Lessons learned, recovery, sales of "we climbed out."
**How**: Comfort → Trigger → Crisis (treasure) → Recovery → Better Place.
**Trade-offs**: Overused can feel formulaic — vary detail.

## No Easy Way
**When to use**: Sceptical or sophisticated audiences.
**How**: Problem → Early Success → Setback → Crisis → Recovery → Better Place.
**Trade-offs**: Longer; honesty about setbacks is the feature.

## Pride & Fall Pre-emption
**When to use**: Audience mentally running "too good to be true."
**How**: Narrate their Icarus fears, then answer with No Easy Way safeguards.
**Trade-offs**: Naming risks can scare pioneers — pair with Innovation Curve.

## Hero & Guide Reframe
**When to use**: Self-centered pitch or brand story.
**How**: Map user journey; pick Guide type and gift; arc with Rags / Hole / No Easy Way.
**Trade-offs**: You get less spotlight — intentional.

## Story Listening (Four Passes)
**When to use**: Need others' expertise/wisdom.
**How**: Emotion → timeline → decisions → novice-vs-expert; then arc it.
**Trade-offs**: Time-intensive; resists "quick interview."

## Show & Tell Washing Line
**When to use**: Any slide presentation.
**How**: Image → name it → tell unseen → new image → re-peg.
**Trade-offs**: Requires disciplined slide design (less text).

## Story Bank Hygiene
**When to use**: Multiple stories in play across a team.
**How**: Log point, audience, prior tellings, ownership, permission, freshness.
**Trade-offs**: Process overhead; prevents trust-breaking mistakes.
