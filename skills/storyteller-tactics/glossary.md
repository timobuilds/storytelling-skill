# Glossary — Storyteller Tactics

**Abstractions** — Observation ladder from action up to rationalisation; don't trust explanations alone (Ch 4).

**Audience Profile** — Structured portrait of listeners: problems, hopes, fears, existing talk/behavior (Ch 9).

**Big / Small / Inside / Outside** — Matrix for matching story scale and audience (Ch 9).

**Circle of Life** — Child/Adult/Parent archetypes across home → world → home (Ch 3).

**Curious Tales** — Curiosity, caution, flow, envy, sacrifice as drivers (Ch 3).

**Curse of knowledge** — Experts can't find the entry point for newcomers (Ch 5).

**Cut to the Chase** — Bailout: jump to action, emotion, or meaning when dull (Ch 5).

**Data Detectives** — Zoomer / Detective / Sceptic ways to story-wrap data (Ch 4).

**Desert Island Cards** — Seven core tactics covering the full process (Ch 1).

**Downfall** — Flawed power → denial → disgrace; defend or exploit (Ch 7).

**Dragon & the City** — Status quo (City) vs threat/opportunity (Dragon); Escape/Defend/Attack (Ch 3).

**Drive Stories** — Autonomy, mastery, purpose stories for team culture (Ch 5).

**Emotional Dashboard** — Strong emotions as indicators of story moments (Ch 4).

**Epic Fail** — Six attribution frames after failure (catharsis, hubris, betrayal, etc.) (Ch 7).

**Five Ts** — Timeline, Turning Points, Tensions, Temptations, Teachable Moments (Ch 7).

**Good & Evil** — Haidt's six moral foundations as story conflict (Ch 3).

**Happy Ever Afters** — Ending via growing up, home/love/respect, or doing right (Ch 7).

**Hero & Guide** — User as Hero; you as Expert Guide with a gift (Ch 5).

**Innovation Curve** — Match stories to pioneer → traditional risk appetite (Ch 7).

**Leave It Out** — Create intrigue by deliberate omission / information gap (Ch 8).

**Man in a Hole** — Comfort → trigger → crisis → recovery → better place (Ch 7).

**Movie Time** — Force a sensory "movie" for abstracts (Ch 8).

**No Easy Way** — Realistic rise/fall arc with setbacks (Ch 7).

**Order & Chaos** — Known vs Unknown worlds; hero balances both (Ch 3).

**Pitch Perfect / POPP** — Problem, Opportunity, Practical Steps, Promise (Ch 6).

**Pride & Fall** — Icarus arc used to pre-empt sceptics (Ch 7).

**Rags to Riches** — Hidden value → struggle → deserved recognition (Ch 7).

**Recipe** — Ordered stack of tactics for a named problem (Ch 2).

**Rolls Royce Moment** — One vivid exemplary relatable detail (Ch 8).

**Rules, Cheats & Rebels** — Attention from rule-breaking; rebel vs cheat morals (Ch 3).

**Secrets & Puzzles** — Information-gap hooks (must deliver) (Ch 3).

**Show & Tell** — Washing-line sync of slides and spoken word (Ch 8).

**Simple Sales Stories** — Someone like you / someone you like buys this (Ch 6).

**Social Proof** — Trends, prototypes, story-testimonials (Ch 4).

**Story Bank** — Catalog of stories + metadata (permission, audience, freshness) (Ch 9).

**Story Building System** — Seven diagnostic questions → tactic category (Ch 1).

**Story Hooks** — Unexpected or knowledge-based attention openers (Ch 8).

**Story Listening** — Four-pass listen for teachable moments (Ch 4).

**Story-ish Conversations** — Prompt everyday talk into visual, emotional, conflict stories (Ch 6).

**That's Funny** — Unexpected insight moments (ha-ha / peculiar / acting funny) (Ch 4).

**Thoughtful Failures** — Blame-free extraction of morals from mistakes (Ch 4).

**Three Great Conflicts** — Hero vs Nature / Society / Self (Ch 3).

**Three is the Magic Number** — Attention, Reversal, or Moderate threes (Ch 8).

**Trust Me, I'm an Expert** — Values-in-action credibility story (Ch 5).

**Universal Stories** — Shared human traits as story foundations (Ch 3).

**Voyage & Return** — Leave home, change in strange world, return transformed (Ch 7).

**What's It About?** — Change / new info / personal benefit one-liners (Ch 5).

**What's My Motivation?** — Stanislavski prompts for actors in the project (Ch 5).
