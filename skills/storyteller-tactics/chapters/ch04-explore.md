# Chapter 4: Explore Tactics

## Core Idea
Explore tactics answer *where to find your story*: listen, observe emotion and behavior, mine data and failures for teachable moments.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Story Listening** (Desert Island; credit: Gary Klein, *Sources of Power*): Four listens for teachable moments.
  1. Memorable story + strong emotion?
  2. Basic timeline (goal → what happened)?
  3. Key decisions + counterfactuals?
  4. Expertise: what would a novice miss?
  - Then shape with No Easy Way or Man in a Hole.

- **Emotional Dashboard**: Emotions are gauges of progress toward goals.
  - Template: "I felt… when I realised… because I wanted… and so I…"
  - Run for yourself *and* for the user.

- **Data Detectives** (credit: Rosling, *Factfulness*): Three modes —
  - **Data Zoomer**: big picture trend ↔ vivid single example.
  - **Data Detective**: body (problem) → evidence → vital clue → killer (solution).
  - **Data Sceptic**: How might we be wrong? What can't be measured?

- **Thoughtful Failures** (credit: Laszlo Bock / Google): Extract wisdom without blame.
  - Probe goals, assumptions, insights, skills, communication.
  - "Find the moral in the mistake, then teach it."
  - Shape with No Easy Way. Contrast with Epic Fail attribution styles.

- **That's Funny** (Asimov + Klein): Unexpected insights as gold.
  - Funny ha-ha (misconception rebuilt), funny peculiar (contradiction/coincidence), acting funny (out of character → What's My Motivation?).

- **Abstractions** (credit: Piaget): Observation ladder — Action → Imitation → Ritual/Play → Narrative → Rationalisation. Contradictions between say/do are insight mines.

- **Social Proof** (credit: Cialdini): In uncertainty, people watch others.
  - Trends (zoom out/in), Prototypes (watch behavior), Testimonials (ask story questions, not "good service").

## Key Concepts
- **Teachable moment**: Decision under pressure that reveals expertise.
- **Social proof**: Others' behavior as persuasive evidence.
- **Abstraction levels**: Behavior is deeper than verbal explanation.

## Mental Models
- Use Story Listening when you need wisdom you don't have.
- Use Emotional Dashboard when you "have no story" — strong feeling = story seed.
- Use Data Detectives when facts aren't sticking.
- Use Social Proof before Pitch Perfect.

## Anti-patterns
- **Interview-only research** without observing action (Abstractions).
- **Blame-focused postmortems** instead of Thoughtful Failures.
- **Dumping charts** without Zoomer or Detective frame.
- **Bland testimonials** ("good service") instead of moment stories.

## Key Takeaways
1. Innovators must be story-listeners, not only tellers.
2. Emotion marks where the story is.
3. Data needs a narrative wrapper to be remembered.
4. Failure stories are assets if you extract the moral.
5. Desert Island Explore card: **Story Listening**.
