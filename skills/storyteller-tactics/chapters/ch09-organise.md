# Chapter 9: Organise Tactics

## Core Idea
Organise tactics answer *how to share your story*: bank stories ethically, match size/audience, and profile listeners before you speak.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Story Bank** (Desert Island): Stories are assets; metadata prevents trust damage.
  - Failure modes without a bank: Boring (told twice), Disrespectful, Gossipy (broke confidence), Stolen, Stale.
  - Track: what / why (point) / who benefits / who already heard / whose story / still current?
  - Prefer shared online space for teams.

- **Big, Small, Inside, Outside**: Matrix for choosing the right story type.

  |  | Inside | Outside |
  |--|--------|---------|
  | **Big** | Induction (Drive Stories), Awards (Rolls Royce Moment) | Pitch (Pitch Perfect), Recruitment (Curious Tales), Conference (Show & Tell) |
  | **Small** | Habit/Training (Emotional Dashboard), Appraisal (Story Listening), Culture (Thoughtful Failures) | Blog/Social (What's It About?), Brand (Hero & Guide) |

  - Big = rehearsed, one-to-many ("why we do this").
  - Small = spontaneous, conversational ("how we do things round here").

- **Audience Profile**: Know who you're talking to before crafting.
  1. Basics + problems + hopes/values + fears + what they already say/do.
  2. Can you tell a "someone like them" story? (Simple Sales Stories)
  3. Who should tell it — boss claim vs customer claim? (Social Proof)
  4. Permission to tell someone else's story? Don't assume; ask. Log in Story Bank.
  5. Deepen with Innovation Curve for reaction-to-newness.

## Key Concepts
- **Story metadata**: Provenance, audience history, freshness, permission.
- **Big vs Small stories**: Rehearsed narrative vs living culture examples.
- **Inside vs Outside**: Colleagues vs market/world.

## Mental Models
- Use Story Bank as soon as you have more than a handful of working stories.
- Use Big/Small/Inside/Outside when choosing *which* story for *which* room.
- Use Audience Profile before any Sell, Pitch, or Motivate recipe.
- Always ask permission before retelling someone else's story.

## Anti-patterns
- **Assuming your interest = their interest**.
- **Retelling confidential or stale stories**.
- **Only big external stories** while culture runs on small inside stories.
- **Boss saying "we're great"** when a customer story would convert.

## Key Takeaways
1. A Story Bank protects trust as much as it aids recall.
2. Most storytelling time is small + inside — invest there.
3. Audience Profile first; craft second.
4. Permission and attribution are non-negotiable.
5. Desert Island Organise card: **Story Bank**.
