# Chapter 1: Story Building System

## Core Idea
Storytelling is a diagnosable process: ask seven questions; wherever you answer "No," that category of tactics is where you start.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Story Building System**: Decision tree that routes you to the right tactic family.
  - When to use: Any time you're stuck or starting a story from scratch.
  - How: Walk the questions top-to-bottom. First "No" → that category.

| Question | If No → |
|----------|---------|
| Do you know why you need a story? | **Concept** |
| Do you know where to find your story? | **Explore** |
| Do you know your role in the story? | **Character** |
| Do you know what your story needs to do? | **Function** |
| Do you know how to plan your story? | **Structure** |
| Do you know how to tell your story? | **Style** |
| Do you know how to share your story? | **Organise** |

- **Category jobs**:
  - **Concept**: Frame work as an epic adventure; shape how people see themselves and the world.
  - **Explore**: Navigate unknown/changing situations; make a map.
  - **Character**: Connect and earn trust; show why we can trust you.
  - **Function**: Put stories to work (more effective than facts alone).
  - **Structure**: Make ideas flow via basic story patterns.
  - **Style**: Make information memorable.
  - **Organise**: Plan how/when/to whom you tell.
  - **Recipe**: Combine tactics to solve a common problem.

- **Three ways into the deck**:
  1. **Pick a card, any card** — random card × 3; force-fit; keep the best.
  2. **Desert Island Cards** — seven "heads of family" covering the whole process.
  3. **Recipe Cards** — pre-combined stacks for Sell / Motivate / Convince / Connect / Explain / Lead / Impress.

## Key Concepts
- **Desert Island Cards**: The Dragon & the City, Story Listening, Trust Me I'm an Expert, Pitch Perfect, Man in a Hole, Movie Time, Story Bank.
- **Recipe**: A problem-solving stack of 4–5 tactics in order.
- **Force-fit**: Make your real story elements fit an arbitrary tactic — constraint breeds creativity.

## Mental Models
- Use the Story Building System when you don't know which card to pick.
- Prefer Desert Island Cards when teaching beginners or working under time pressure.
- Prefer Recipes when the outcome is clear (sell, motivate, pitch) but the path isn't.

## Anti-patterns
- **Jumping to Style before Concept/Explore**: Pretty telling of an empty story.
- **Treating cards as optional fluff**: Each card is a named method with steps — follow the steps.
- **One card forever**: Stories improve by stacking tactics (Recipe thinking).

## Key Takeaways
1. Diagnose first: seven questions → one category.
2. Seven Desert Island Cards cover the full process if you learn nothing else.
3. Recipes are the fastest path from problem → finished story.
4. Random force-fit is a legitimate creativity technique.
5. Organise last: collect, match audience, share ethically.
