# Chapter 2: Recipe Cards

## Core Idea
Recipes combine 4–5 tactics in sequence to solve a named business problem. Don't invent a stack when a recipe already exists.

## Frameworks Introduced

### Stories that Sell — Show your value
**Job**: Convince people you can deliver. Stories beat spreadsheets for trust.
1. **Audience Profile** — Who are they? What problems are you solving?
2. **Simple Sales Stories** — Show how you've helped someone like them.
3. **Social Proof** — Trends, prototypes, testimonials.
4. **Rags to Riches** — Optimistic arc with customer at the heart.
5. **Pitch Perfect** — Boil down to elevator pitch.

### Stories that Motivate — Make people act
**Job**: Get people behind you; persuade a leap of faith.
1. **The Dragon & the City** — Big-picture goal (city vs dragon).
2. **Drive Stories** — What's motivating you (and might motivate us).
3. **Three Great Conflicts** — Barriers you must overcome.
4. **Innovation Curve** — Reassure about risk by adopter type.
5. **No Easy Way** — Realistic journey ahead.

### Stories that Convince — Explain your expertise
**Job**: Get non-experts to trust your judgment without data overload.
1. **Three is the Magic Number** — How many facts can they remember?
2. **That's Funny** — Share the excitement of your insights.
3. **Data Detectives** — Wrap key facts in story form.
4. **Trust Me, I'm an Expert** — Show character/values in action.
5. **Hero & Guide** — You as Expert Guide helping them.

### Stories that Connect — Get closer to people
**Job**: Build empathy so you can build better ideas/products/teams.
1. **Story Listening** — Gather wisdom from others' stories.
2. **Abstractions** — Observe behavior, don't only ask questions.
3. **Universal Stories** — Shared human traits as building blocks.
4. **Story-ish Conversations** — Find stories in everyday talk.
5. **Circle of Life** — Relatable archetypes and life stages.

### Stories that Explain — Clarify your goals
**Job**: Bring strategy to life so people understand and care.
1. **Order & Chaos** — Where you stand in a changing world.
2. **Good & Evil** — Which side in important moral battles.
3. **What's It About?** — Strategy in one clear line.
4. **Rolls Royce Moment** — One vivid exemplary detail.
5. **Story Hooks** — Make strategy interesting enough to read.

### Stories that Lead — Show the way
**Job**: Build team culture via shared struggles, triumphs, setbacks.
1. **Curious Tales** — What makes teammates tick.
2. **Man in a Hole** — Frame work as epic journey with lessons.
3. **Emotional Dashboard** — Highs/lows as story sources.
4. **Thoughtful Failures** — Extract wisdom from mistakes.
5. **Story Bank** — Collect reusable team stories.

### Stories that Impress — Nail that presentation
**Job**: Present with confidence; hook attention; show and tell.
1. **Movie Time** — Ensure you're telling a story, not dumping facts.
2. **Five Ts** — Beats of an off-the-cuff story structure.
3. **Story Hooks** — Grab attention (don't bore with slides).
4. **Show & Tell** — Align visuals and spoken word.
5. **Cut to the Chase** — Bailout plan if the room goes cold.

## Key Concepts
- **Recipe order matters**: Profile before pitch; listen before lead.
- **Ingredient reuse**: Same tactic appears in multiple recipes with different jobs.

## Mental Models
- Use a Recipe when the outcome is clear but composition isn't.
- Use Sell for trust/conversion; Motivate for change adoption; Convince for expertise; Connect for research/empathy; Explain for strategy; Lead for culture; Impress for presentations.

## Anti-patterns
- **Skipping Audience Profile** in Sell/Motivate recipes.
- **Pitching facts without Trust Me / Social Proof**.
- **Presentation = slide dump** without Movie Time + Show & Tell.

## Key Takeaways
1. Seven recipes cover the most common storytelling jobs.
2. Follow the numbered order — later cards depend on earlier ones.
3. Recipes are stacks, not menus: do all steps, then edit.
4. Cross-link to chapter files for the full how-to of each ingredient.
