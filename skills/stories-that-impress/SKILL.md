# Stories that Impress

## Doctrine

Read [../storyteller-tactics/references/doctrine.md](../storyteller-tactics/references/doctrine.md). Job: **present with confidence** — hook attention, flow like a story, master Show & Tell. Prefer having a Sell/Motivate/Explain draft first when content is empty.

## Draft-first

Propose a filled [assets/impress-story-brief.md](assets/impress-story-brief.md). Match density/shape of [assets/exemplar-impress-story-brief.md](assets/exemplar-impress-story-brief.md) (fictional — don’t copy its facts). Mark `TBD`. Ask them to react.

## Process

1. **Movie Time** — action + emotion → meaning; movie-check every beat. (ch08)
2. **Five Ts** — Timeline, Turning Points, Tensions, Temptations, Teachable Moment. (ch07)
3. **Story Hooks** — unexpected or knowledge openers (must pay off). (ch08)
4. **Show & Tell** — washing line: image → name it → tell unseen → re-peg. Never read slides. (ch08)
5. **Cut to the Chase** — bailout plan: cut to action / emotion / meaning, or ask a question. (ch05)
6. Cap at three memorable points; optional Icebreaker warm-up (ch06).

## Output

Filled brief + talk track outline + slide Show/Tell map (not full slide design unless asked).

## Anti-patterns

- Slide dump / reading the slide
- Style polish with no underlying story job
- No bailout plan when the room goes cold
