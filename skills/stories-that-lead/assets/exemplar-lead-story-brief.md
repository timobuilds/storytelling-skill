Product / startup themed. Composite — not a real company.

```markdown
# Lead Story Brief
- Team / audience (inside): Harbor product squad (PM + eng + design, 11 people) after a messy public beta.
- Curious Tales insight (what makes them tick): Flow shows up in war-room pairing after incidents — people light up fixing real user exceptions; they go flat in roadmap reviews that reward certainty theater.
- Man in a Hole beats (shared journey):
  - Comfort: “Design partners forgive; ship the beta.”
  - Trigger: Public beta broke invite permissions; support volume 5×; Twitter thread.
  - Crisis: Two design partners paused; trust dent with the board narrative.
  - Recovery: Blameless timeline + permission matrix tests before merge + “no silent launch” rule.
  - Better Place: We still ship weekly — but “ready” means adversary paths, not demo paths.
- Emotional Dashboard moments: Shame (on-call) → relief (first clean canary) → pride (partner: “you told us before the thread did”).
- Thoughtful Failure (moral + who else needs it): Moral: we optimized for demoable invite happy path, not adversary sharing. Who else: GTM still demoing the old happy path — they need the new script this week.
- Story Bank entries:
  - Story keywords: beta-permissions / blameless / canary / no-silent-launch
  - Point: Speed without adversary tests is borrowed trust
  - Who it’s for: new hires; pre-launch reviews; GTM demos
  - Already told to: company all-hands 2026-02-01
  - Whose story / permission: squad composite; no individual blame names
  - Fresh?: Yes — still shapes our launch checklist
- Surface (induction / habit / appraisal / all-hands): Induction + pre-launch habit checklist
- Draft culture story:
  “We used to treat ‘partners forgive’ as a feature. Then beta invites broke and support lit up. The hole wasn’t ‘we’re bad builders’ — it was shipping a demo path without an adversary path. Climb-out was boring on purpose: timeline, tests, canary, no silent launches. Better place: we still move fast, but ready means permission tests. If you’re new here, that’s what ‘how we build’ means.”
```
