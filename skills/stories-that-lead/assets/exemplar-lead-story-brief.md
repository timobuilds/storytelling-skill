# Exemplar — Lead Story Brief (fictional)

Composite example for agents. Not a real company.

```markdown
# Lead Story Brief
- Team / audience (inside): 18-person eng + design squad at “Notebridge” (collab docs startup). New EM inherited a team that ships scared after a bad launch.
- Curious Tales insight (what makes them tick): Flow shows up in pair-debugging after incidents — people light up solving puzzles together; they go flat in status meetings that reward certainty theater.
- Man in a Hole beats (shared journey):
  - Comfort: “We move fast; users forgive.”
  - Trigger: Launch broke sharing permissions; support volume 4×.
  - Crisis: Public workaround thread; trust dent with design partners.
  - Recovery: Blameless timeline + permission matrix tests before merge.
  - Better Place: We still ship weekly — but “green” means permission tests, not vibes.
- Emotional Dashboard moments: Shame (on-call) → relief (first clean canary) → pride (design partner said “you told us before Twitter did”).
- Thoughtful Failure (moral + who else needs it): Moral: we optimized for demo-able sharing, not adversary sharing. Who else: sales engineers still demoing the old happy path — they need the new script this week.
- Story Bank entries:
  - Story keywords: permissions-launch / blameless / canary
  - Point: Speed without adversary tests is borrowed trust
  - Who it’s for: new hires; pre-launch reviews
  - Already told to: eng all-hands 2026-02-01
  - Whose story / permission: team composite; no individual blame names
  - Fresh?: Yes — still shapes our checklist
- Surface (induction / habit / appraisal / all-hands): Induction + pre-launch habit checklist
- Draft culture story:
  “We used to treat ‘users forgive’ as a feature. Then sharing permissions broke and support lit up like a switchboard. The hole wasn’t ‘we’re bad engineers’ — it was shipping a demo path without an adversary path. Climb-out was boring on purpose: timeline, tests, canary. Better place: we still move fast, but green means permission tests. If you’re new here, that’s what ‘how we work’ means.”
- TBD: Add SE demo script link to Story Bank.
```
